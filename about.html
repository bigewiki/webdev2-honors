About

How I Got Here

Thank you for visiting my site! I’m Edward, I work at a web hosting company and specialize in website migrations. I started, first, taking tech support calls before making my way into a tier 2 technical position supporting virtual and dedicated private web servers. Here I learned a lot more about Linux before I was pulled to work with support teams skilled in WordPress. My interest and investment in programming and web development grew as I learned Javascript and Bash Shell Scripting. During this time I found my calling and knew I wanted to someday become a developer. After a couple failed interview attempts I finally got promoted to join a Professional Services team and have spent most of my tenure running the team as the lead.

Over the next couple years with Professional Services I took the opportunity to apply my knowledge to create scripts and tools to simplify and automate our workflow. I built a database and API to support tracking and reporting and fed that data into a dashboard that served to replace the old spreadsheets that measured our productivity previously.

As my progression toward becoming a developer started to stagnate I began taking my education more seriously. I transitioned from being completely self-taught to taking on course work at Maricopa Community Colleges. I started at Gateway before moving on to Mesa, but will finish my degree in Scottsdale as they have the degree program I’ve had my eye on. In the meantime I’m fortunate enough to have the opportunity to apply what I’ve learned in my field.

After creating another internal application that guided sales and support through qualifying and quoting our service I started to gain some recognition for my efforts and got the chance to work on a larger initiative to build a system that would serve our customers. For the past several months I’ve had the humbling experience of working with an engineering team to make this all a reality. I’ve learned a lot but have much further to go. I wholeheartedly believe that “the more you learn, the less you know”.

I’m an avid supporter of WordPress and attend WordCamp, the conferences hosted in most major cities, annually. Shown in the image are the iconic WordPress lanyards they give out with the passes at each event. I wanted to build a site about something that I would consider myself an expert in, WordPress migrations would tick that box.
