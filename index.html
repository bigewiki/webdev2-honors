Migrating WordPress

The beauty of using WordPress as your Content Management System (CMS) is that it's open-source, the base software is free, and you have the freedom to do almost anything with it. A major benefit of using WordPress, or another open-source CMS, such as Joomla, Drupal, or Magento, is that, since it’s not proprietary to the hosting platform, the site can be transferred at will.

Migrating your first WordPress site can be a challenge. There’s a lot that can go wrong, even if you’ve done everything right. My goal, as a professional migration specialist, is to arm you with the knowledge you need to attempt your own WordPress migration. It’s not that hard once you understand the basics.

If you’re somewhat seasoned and want to take your migration game to the next level, I hope to give you some guidance on what tools and methods are at your disposal.

Novice

Beginners should take some time to understand the basic structure of their WordPress site, DNS, what to expect during migration, and how to preview their site before it goes live. I'll also introduce some easy-to-use plugins that make the effort simpler and less painful.

Intermediate

If you already know the basics, are acquainted, or want to learn File Transfer Protocol (FTP) and some of the slightly more advanced interfaces in your hosting control panel this is the place to start. I’ll go over the basics of how to use the intermediate level tools to migrate you WordPress website.

Advanced

If you're familiar with the Linux command-line interface (CLI) and your host gives you secure-shell (SSH) access this is the place to start. In order to quickly, easily, and reliably migrate and configure sites professionally we employ command-line scripts and functions and live inside our terminals. Here, I can share what utilities we use and how you can use them too.
